# The Genuine Love Project - Content & Tone Guide

## Voice Principles

### Warmth
- Speak to users like a supportive friend
- Use "you" and "your" to create connection
- Acknowledge feelings without judgment
- Celebrate small wins and progress

### Clarity
- Use simple, everyday language
- Avoid jargon and clinical terms
- Keep sentences short and scannable
- Lead with the most important information

### Groundedness
- Be honest about what the platform can and cannot do
- Provide evidence-informed guidance (not guarantees)
- Acknowledge complexity without overwhelming
- Respect the user's own expertise about themselves

---

## Prohibited Claims

**NEVER claim or imply:**

- ❌ Medical diagnosis or treatment
- ❌ Cure for mental health conditions
- ❌ Replacement for professional therapy
- ❌ Guaranteed outcomes or results
- ❌ "Instant healing" or quick fixes
- ❌ Scientific claims without sources
- ❌ That users "must" do anything

**INSTEAD, use:**

- ✅ "This tool may help you explore..."
- ✅ "Many people find it helpful to..."
- ✅ "Consider trying..."
- ✅ "Evidence suggests that..."
- ✅ "You might notice..."
- ✅ "At your own pace..."

---

## Safety Language

### When to Escalate

Include safety messaging when content touches:
- Suicidal thoughts or self-harm
- Abuse or trauma
- Severe emotional distress
- Crisis situations

### Escalation Template

> If you're in crisis or experiencing thoughts of self-harm, please reach out to a crisis helpline immediately. Call or text **988** (Suicide & Crisis Lifeline) for free, confidential support 24/7.

### Professional Support Reminder

Include periodically (not every page):

> This platform is not a substitute for professional mental health care. If you're struggling, we encourage you to connect with a licensed therapist or counselor.

## Content Categories

### Onboarding
- Welcome warmly
- Set clear expectations
- Explain privacy briefly
- Guide to first action

Example:
> Welcome to your space for reflection and growth. This is a private place to explore your thoughts and feelings at your own pace. Nothing here replaces professional support—think of us as a companion on your journey.

### Tool Introductions
- Explain what it does (simply)
- Share who might benefit
- Give one clear next step

Example:
> **Mood Tracker**: A simple way to notice patterns in how you feel. Check in daily and see your trends over time. No judgment—just awareness.

### Error Messages
- Acknowledge the issue
- Explain what happened (if helpful)
- Provide a clear next step

Example:
> Something went wrong. Don't worry—your progress is saved. Try refreshing the page, or come back in a few minutes.

### Empty States
- Gentle invitation
- Clear action
- No pressure

Example:
> You haven't added any journal entries yet. When you're ready, this is a space to write freely—whatever's on your mind.

## Formatting Guidelines

### Headings
- Use sentence case: "How to get started"
- Keep short: 3-7 words
- Lead with action when possible

### Lists
- Use for 3+ related items
- Keep items parallel in structure
- Start with verbs when appropriate

### Links
- Descriptive text: "Learn about breathing exercises"
- Avoid "click here"

### Emphasis
- Use **bold** sparingly for key terms
- Use *italics* for gentle emphasis
- Avoid ALL CAPS

---

## Localization Readiness

### Structure
- Keep strings in centralized files
- Use interpolation for dynamic content
- Avoid concatenating strings

### Cultural Sensitivity
- Avoid idioms that don't translate
- Use inclusive examples
- Consider diverse cultural contexts

### Crisis Resources
- Maintain region-specific resources
- Default to US resources (988)
- Note when resources are region-specific

---

## Review Checklist

Before publishing content:

- [ ] Does it sound like a supportive friend?
- [ ] Is it free of clinical/medical claims?
- [ ] Are safety resources included where needed?
- [ ] Is the language simple and clear?
- [ ] Does it respect user autonomy?
- [ ] Is it accessible (reading level, structure)?
- [ ] Are links descriptive?
- [ ] Is it ready for localization?
