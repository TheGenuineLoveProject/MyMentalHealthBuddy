# Ethical Amplification Guide

## What Was Created

Five narrative artifacts — short, self-contained text descriptions of The Genuine Love Project.
They are stored in `docs/NARRATIVE_ARTIFACTS.md`.

Each artifact:
- Is 50–120 words
- Stands alone without links or context
- Describes what the platform is, not what it makes you
- Contains no calls to action beyond "It's here if you want it."
- Makes no outcome claims, healing promises, or transformation language

The artifacts are:
1. **What It Is** — factual description of tools and privacy model
2. **Who It Might Be For** — self-recognition for quiet processors
3. **What It Does Not Do** — explicit boundary-setting (no diagnosis, no tracking, no guilt)
4. **How It Works** — walkthrough of the experience with emphasis on no-pressure design
5. **Why It Exists** — philosophy: attention returned, not captured

## Canonical Entry Point

The sole public destination is the landing page at `/`.
Routes `/index` and `/main` redirect there.
No secondary funnels, alternate CTAs, or deep-link campaigns exist.

The landing page headline reads:
> "A Private Space for Honest Reflection"

This matches implemented behavior exactly. The platform is private,
it contains reflection tools, and users control their own pace.

## Where Artifacts May Be Shared

- Personal social media posts by the project owner
- Direct messages to someone who asks "what is this?"
- A bio link description
- A pinned post or "about" section
- A reply when someone asks for wellness tool recommendations

## Where Artifacts Must NOT Be Shared

- Paid advertising (search, social, display, influencer)
- Affiliate or referral programs
- Email campaigns to non-users
- Retargeting or remarketing
- Any context that implies urgency, scarcity, or limited availability
- Wellness forums or communities as unsolicited promotion
- Any platform where sharing creates an engagement metric (likes, shares, comments)
  that feeds back into a growth loop

## How to Tell If Amplification Has Gone Too Far

Ask these questions. If the answer to any is "yes," stop.

1. **Am I trying to reach people who haven't asked?**
   Amplification shares with people who are already looking.
   It does not interrupt people who aren't.

2. **Am I measuring how many people saw this?**
   Ethical amplification has no analytics on the artifacts themselves.
   If you're tracking impressions, opens, or click-through rates on the artifacts,
   you've crossed into marketing.

3. **Am I optimizing the language for conversion?**
   The artifacts describe what exists. If you're A/B testing them
   or rewriting them to "perform better," they are no longer artifacts.
   They are ads.

4. **Does sharing this create pressure to engage?**
   If someone reads an artifact and feels they *should* sign up,
   the artifact has failed. The correct response is:
   "Oh, that exists. Maybe I'll look at it sometime."

5. **Am I sharing this to grow the platform?**
   Ethical amplification lets truth travel. It does not pursue growth.
   If the motivation is user acquisition, that is marketing,
   regardless of how gentle the language is.
