# Genuine Love Brand Lexicon

**Version:** 1.0  
**Last Updated:** December 2024

## Core Philosophy

The Genuine Love Project uses **neutral, observation-based language** that honors human dignity and autonomy. We never instruct, diagnose, promise healing, or imply deficiency.

---

## Language Principles

### 1. Invitation Over Instruction
**Always frame as choices, never commands.**

| Avoid | Use Instead |
|-------|-------------|
| "You should..." | "You might consider..." |
| "Try this" | "If you'd like, you could..." |
| "Do this exercise" | "Here's something you could explore" |
| "Follow these steps" | "Some people find it helpful to..." |

### 2. Observation Over Judgment
**Describe without evaluating.**

| Avoid | Use Instead |
|-------|-------------|
| "Bad mood" | "Low energy state" |
| "Negative thoughts" | "Heavy thoughts" |
| "You're depressed" | "You may be carrying something heavy" |
| "Anxiety attack" | "Heightened activation" |
| "Fix your thinking" | "Notice your patterns" |

### 3. Neutrality Over Pathology
**Use descriptive, non-clinical language.**

| Clinical Term | Neutral Alternative |
|---------------|---------------------|
| Mood tracking | State noticing |
| Symptoms | Signals / Patterns |
| Treatment | Support / Practice |
| Disorder | Experience / Pattern |
| Recovery | Integration / Growth |
| Therapy | Reflection / Exploration |

### 4. Agency Over Authority
**Users are the experts on themselves.**

| Avoid | Use Instead |
|-------|-------------|
| "This will help you" | "Some find this supportive" |
| "Research shows..." | "One perspective suggests..." |
| "The right way is..." | "One approach you might try..." |
| "You need to..." | "You're welcome to..." |

## Standard Disclaimers

### Reflection Tools
> "This is a reflection tool for journaling support, not medical advice or diagnosis. If you feel unsafe, please seek immediate help."

### AI Responses
> "You know yourself best. Take what serves you, leave what doesn't."

### Insight Cards
> "Not a diagnosis — just supportive reflection to help you feel grounded."

### End of Flows
> "Please ignore anything that doesn't feel accurate or helpful."

## State Tracker Vocabulary

We observe states across 6 dimensions using **non-judgmental labels**:

| Dimension | Low End | High End |
|-----------|---------|----------|
| Energy | Depleted | Wired |
| Clarity | Foggy | Sharp |
| Openness | Closed | Expansive |
| Regulation | Reactive | Grounded |
| Presence | Distant | Absorbed |
| Pace | Rushed | Still |

**Note:** Both ends of each spectrum are neutral observations, not goals. "Depleted" is not bad; "Wired" is not good. They're simply descriptions of current state.

## Forbidden Language

The following patterns are **never** used in the platform:

1. **Diagnostic language**
   - "You have..." / "You are..." (followed by condition)
   - "This indicates..." / "This means..."
   - Any DSM terminology

2. **Imperative commands**
   - "You must..." / "You need to..."
   - "Always..." / "Never..."
   - "Stop doing..." / "Start doing..."

3. **Promises**
   - "This will heal you"
   - "Guaranteed to work"
   - "Cure your..."

4. **Deficit framing**
   - "What's wrong with you"
   - "Your problem is..."
   - "You're broken"
   - "You need to fix..."

### Values Alignment

## Crisis Response Language

When crisis keywords are detected, the response must:

1. **Validate without amplifying**
   - "It sounds like you're carrying something very heavy right now."

2. **Offer resources without commanding**
   - "If you'd like support, these resources are available..."

3. **Maintain dignity**
   - "You deserve support right now."

4. **End with agency**
   - "You're the expert on what you need."

---

## AI Guardrails Summary

All AI responses must:
- Maintain 80%+ question ratio
- End with "You know yourself best"
- Never instruct, diagnose, promise, or imply deficiency
- Allow user disagreement
- Include appropriate disclaimer
