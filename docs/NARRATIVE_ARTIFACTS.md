# Narrative Artifacts

Five self-contained text descriptions of The Genuine Love Project.
Each can be shared independently. None require context, links, or follow-up.

---

## Artifact 1: What It Is

A private space for journaling, mood tracking, and AI-assisted reflection.
No social features. No public profile. No one sees what you write.
The tools are grounded in evidence-informed approaches — IFS, polyvagal theory,
attachment science, DBT, and ACT principles — presented as education, not treatment.
Core tools are free. There is a paid tier if you want more.
It's here if you want it.

---

## Artifact 2: Who It Might Be For

Some people process things quietly. They read about attachment styles at 2 AM.
They journal but never show anyone. They want to understand why they react the
way they do, but they're not ready to say that out loud.
This was built for that kind of person.
It's here if you want it.

---

## Artifact 3: What It Does Not Do

It does not diagnose. It does not treat. It does not replace therapy.
It does not track your engagement or reward you for logging in.
It does not share your data, show your activity to others,
or send you notifications to come back.
It is a set of tools. You use them when you want to.

---

## Artifact 4: How It Works

You create an account. You get access to journaling, mood tracking,
a reflective AI companion, and educational content grounded in
trauma-informed psychology. Everything is private by default.
If you stop using it, nothing happens. No emails asking where you went.
No streak broken. No guilt. The tools stay available.
It's here if you want it.

---

## Artifact 5: Why It Exists

Most wellness apps want your attention. This one tries to give it back.
The tools are built around how you actually feel,
not how a product wants you to engage.
There is no feed. There is no community. There is no leaderboard.
Just a quiet set of tools for honest self-reflection.
It's here if you want it.
