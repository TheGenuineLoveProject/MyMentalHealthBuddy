# Content Style Guide - The Genuine Love Project

## Overview
This document defines the content standards, approved language, and banned phrases for all platform copy. All content must be educational, non-clinical, trauma-informed, and consent-based.

## Core Principles

### 1. Educational Only
- We provide educational wellness tools, not medical or mental health treatment
- All content is for personal reflection and self-directed learning
- No diagnosis, no treatment claims, no medical guarantees

### 2. Trauma-Informed Language
- Always inviting, never commanding
- Default to gentle
- Never promise outcomes
- Replace "must" with "you can / if you'd like / when you're ready"
- Keep it short: 2–8 words where possible

### 3. Consent-Based
- Every interaction respects user autonomy
- Pause/stop options on every practice
- Opt-out-first design
- Clear benefits before commitment

---

## Banned Phrases (NEVER Use)

### Clinical/Medical Claims
- "cures"
- "treats" 
- "heals"
- "guarantees"
- "clinically proven"
- "therapy" / "therapist"
- "diagnosis"
- "treatment"
- "symptoms"
- "disorder"
- "condition"

### Pressure/Manipulation
- "You must..."
- "You need to..."
- "You should..."
- "Don't miss out"
- "Limited time"
- "Last chance"
- "Act now"
- Shame-based language
- Fear-based urgency

### False Promises
- "Will change your life"
- "Guaranteed results"
- "Fix your problems"
- "Transform instantly"
- "Cure your anxiety"

### Coercive NLP
- "Subconscious reprogramming"
- Embedded commands
- Hypnosis claims
- "Reprogram your mind"

## Approved Language Patterns

### Allowed Outcome Language
- "may help"
- "some people find"
- "often used for"
- "you might notice"
- "can support"
- "designed to help you explore"

### Consent Cues
- "You're in control."
- "Pause or stop anytime."
- "Only if it feels okay."
- "Choose what feels right."
- "Switch to something gentler."

### Encouragement (Non-Clinical)
- "That makes sense."
- "You're not alone in this."
- "Let's aim for one kind step."
- "Even one honest sentence counts."
- "Small steps matter."

### Boundaries
- "What would self-respect look like here?"
- "What's a 'good enough' boundary for today?"
- "You can care and still say no."
- "Your needs matter too."

### Non-Addictive Progress
- "Consistency over intensity."
- "Gentle momentum is still momentum."
- "Skip days are allowed."
- "There's no penalty for taking breaks."
- "Your rhythm matters more than streaks."

## 6 Benefit Pillars

Use these consistently across Home, Pricing, Onboarding, Dashboard, Tools, Share Cards:

1. **Calm & Regulation** - steady body + mind
2. **Clarity & Insight** - name what's real
3. **Confidence & Agency** - one next step
4. **Connection & Support** - belonging, community prompts
5. **Consistency & Growth** - gentle routines, progress snapshots
6. **Meaning & Purpose** - values + identity

---

## The 4-Line Benefit Block

Include on every page header:
- **What you'll get:** (pick 1–2 pillars)
- **Time:** 30–90 seconds
- **Control:** "Pause or stop anytime."
- **Note:** "Educational wellness support — not medical advice. If you're in crisis, visit /crisis."

## Anti-Liability Proof (Process Proof, Not Outcome Proof)

- "Evidence-informed, written in original language (no plagiarism)."
- "Accessible calm design (WCAG AA), sensory-respecting motion."

## Ethical NLP Patterns (Allowed)

- **Reframe:** "Another way to see this is…"
- **Identity:** "I'm becoming someone who practices ___."
- **Future pace:** "If this helped a little, what would be different tomorrow?"
- **Precision:** "What exactly would 'better' look like in one observable way?"

### NOT Allowed
- Hypnosis claims
- "Subconscious reprogramming" guarantees
- Embedded commands / coercive persuasion
- Clinical promises

---

## Error/Empty States (Calm)

- "Nothing is broken. Let's try again."
- "Not loaded yet—refresh or come back in a minute."
- "No entries yet. Want a tiny start?"

---

## Safe Disclaimers (Short + Consistent)

- "Educational support — not medical advice."
- "If you're in crisis, visit /crisis."
- "Self-directed tools for personal reflection."
