# Emotional Outcomes

> **Phase:** B1 · **Mode:** GOVERNANCE / POSITIONING ONLY (no runtime code).
> **Governed by:** MMHB v7.4 Archival Kernel. Educational only — no diagnosis, no clinical outcome claims. Trauma-informed, consent-based, non-coercive. Crisis routing preserved.

This document defines the **emotional outcomes** MMHB orients toward and how they connect to *descriptive, evidence-informed* indicators — never clinical measurement of a person.

---

## 1. Outcome law

Outcomes are **directions of support**, not promises. They are described qualitatively and confidence-honestly. No outcome implies treatment, cure, or clinical change. Distress always routes to crisis resources, never to an "outcome metric."

---

## 2. Target emotional outcomes

- **Felt safety** — the person feels the space is gentle and on their side.
- **Self-compassion** — a kinder inner voice over time.
- **Regulation** — more access to calm; a wider window of tolerance.
- **Clarity** — naming feelings and noticing patterns.
- **Connection** — less alone; supported.
- **Agency & hope** — a sense of choice and forward possibility.

---

## 3. Measurable wellbeing indicators (descriptive, opt-in)

Indicators are **self-reported and reflective**, mapped to TI signals (`../transformation/transformationSignals.md`). They inform the *person's own* reflection, never a grade.

| Outcome | Descriptive indicator | Source (read-only, consented) |
|---|---|---|
| Felt safety | self-noted ease using the app | onboarding/check-in (opt-in) |
| Self-compassion | kinder self-talk in entries | journaling reflection |
| Regulation | self-noted time-to-calm; tool use | breathing/grounding engagement |
| Clarity | range of named emotions; pattern naming | mood + reflection |
| Connection | opt-in reaching-out / supported notes | self-report |
| Agency & hope | small aligned actions; forward language | values/goals reflection |

> **No false precision:** qualitative bands (early / emerging / consistent), never clinical scores or normative comparison.

---

## 4. Authenticity metrics

How we keep outcomes *honest* rather than vanity:

- **Self-validation over engagement** — the truest signal is the person's own "this helped / this fit," not clicks or session time.
- **Consistency of self-report** — gentle corroboration across the person's own reflections, not surveillance.
- **Correction welcomed** — "this doesn't fit" suppresses and down-weights a reflection (anti-overclaiming).
- **Sparse-data honesty** — little data → softer, fewer claims ("it's early").
- **No proxy gaming** — indicators may never be tuned to inflate engagement or conversion.

---

## 5. Emotional regulation metrics (descriptive)

- **Time-to-calm** — self-noted softening after distress (tool-supported).
- **Window of tolerance** — gentle reflection on range the person can stay present with.
- **Tool engagement** — voluntary grounding/breathing use (never feature-gated by it).
- **Co-regulation** — opt-in noting of support/connection.

**Hard rule:** regulation metrics never gate features, drive purchases, or imply a person is "not ready." Distress → support and resources, always.

---

## 6. Guardrails

- Educational only; no clinical/diagnostic claim.
- No business content on emotional-outcome surfaces (Primary Law).
- Consent-gated, reversible, private; WCAG AA.
- Crisis routing on every wellness surface.
