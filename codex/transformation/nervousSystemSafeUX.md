# Nervous-System-Safe UX

> **Phase:** T1 · **Mode:** GOVERNANCE / ARCHITECTURE ONLY (no runtime code).
> **Governed by:** MMHB v7.4 Archival Kernel. Trauma-informed, consent-based, WCAG AA. Educational only — no diagnosis, no clinical automation. Crisis routing preserved on every wellness surface.

This document defines the **interaction rules** for any Transformation Intelligence surface so it supports — never overrides — a person's nervous system. The guiding question for every TI element: *does this help the person feel safer, calmer, and more in choice?* If not, it does not ship.

---

## 1. Nervous-system-safe UX rules

- **Consent before reflection** — TI shows nothing derived until the person explicitly opts in; off by default for sensitive (embodiment) data.
- **Pacing & titration** — small, optional doses of reflection. No walls of analysis. The person controls depth ("show more" is always a choice, never automatic).
- **Calm by default** — gentle motion, soft contrast, generous spacing. Honor `prefers-reduced-motion`; no flashing, no sudden movement, no autoplay.
- **Always an exit** — every TI surface has an obvious, one-tap way to dismiss, pause, or turn off TI entirely.
- **No surprise depth** — heavy or potentially activating reflections are gated behind a clear, optional choice with a soft preview.
- **Resting is celebrated, not penalized** — "you're taking a pause" is shown warmly; no decay shaming.
- **Crisis-first** — `/crisis`, 988, and Crisis Text 741741 are reachable from every wellness surface; an explicit self-harm signal overrides all TI and routes to resources immediately.
- **Accessibility** — WCAG AA contrast, keyboard navigability, screen-reader labels, and reduced-motion compliance are mandatory, not optional.
- **Plain, kind language** — descriptive and self-authored; never clinical, never judgmental, never urgent.

---

## 2. Anti-manipulation rules

TI must be **incapable** of the following by design:

- **No dark patterns** — no confirm-shaming, no disguised ads, no forced continuity, no roach-motel flows.
- **No loss-framing** — never "don't break your streak," "you'll lose progress," or "you're falling behind."
- **No artificial urgency/scarcity** — no countdowns, fake limited-time reflection, or FOMO mechanics.
- **No variable-reward compulsion loops** — no slot-machine reveals or intermittent reinforcement to drive return.
- **No engagement-maximizing optimization** — success is the person's wellbeing and sense of agency, never session time, DAU, or conversion.
- **No business contamination** — zero pricing, upsell, or conversion content on any healing/TI surface (Primary Law).
- **No covert profiling** — derived reflections are for the person only; never sold, shared, or repurposed for persuasion.
- **No coercive defaults** — defaults favor privacy, calm, and less data, not more.

---

## 3. Non-coercive notification system

If TI ever surfaces notifications (future phase), they obey a strict contract:

- **Opt-in only** — silent by default; the person chooses if, what, and when.
- **Person-set cadence** — frequency and quiet hours are user-controlled; no system-imposed nagging.
- **Invitation, not pressure** — "whenever you're ready, there's a gentle reflection waiting" — never "you haven't checked in, come back."
- **No guilt, no streak-bait** — notifications never reference broken streaks, missed days, or lost progress.
- **No urgency styling** — no red badges-of-shame, no alarming counts, no buzz-for-attention.
- **Easy, total off-switch** — one tap to mute or disable, fully reversible, with no dark-pattern friction.
- **Crisis exception** — the only ever-prioritized message type is crisis support, surfaced as resources, never as pressure.
- **Frequency cap** — hard upper bound regardless of settings; TI errs toward silence.

---

## 4. AI role boundaries

What the TI/AI layer **is** and **is not** allowed to do:

**The AI may:**
- Mirror the person's own words and surface patterns *they* can choose to explore.
- Offer optional, evidence-informed, non-clinical reflection prompts.
- Point to existing in-app tools and to crisis resources.

**The AI must never:**
- Diagnose, assess, or imply a clinical condition or outcome.
- Give treatment, medical, legal, or crisis-clinical advice.
- Persuade, upsell, or optimize for engagement/conversion.
- Make decisions *for* the person or take automated account actions based on reflections.
- Claim certainty, authority over the person's experience, or replace human care.
- Continue normal flow when an explicit self-harm signal appears — it must route to crisis resources (BHCE) and defer to humans.

**Stance:** the AI is a *gentle mirror and librarian*, not a coach, therapist, or judge. When uncertain, it asks one clarifying question or defers — it never guesses about a person's safety.
