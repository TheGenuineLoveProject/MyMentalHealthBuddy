# Non-Manipulative Engagement

> **Phase:** B1 · **Mode:** GOVERNANCE / ARCHITECTURE ONLY (no runtime code).
> **Governed by:** MMHB v7.4 Archival Kernel. Educational only — no diagnosis. Trauma-informed, consent-based, non-coercive, WCAG AA. Crisis routing preserved. No business logic in healing flows.

This document defines the **engagement ethics** for MMHB: how the platform earns presence through genuine value and respect, and is *incapable by design* of addiction mechanics.

---

## 1. Engagement law

**Success = the person's wellbeing and agency, never time-on-app.** Engagement is a *byproduct of being genuinely helpful*, never a goal pursued through pressure. If a mechanic would not feel kind to a person in distress, it does not ship.

---

## 2. Anti-addiction UX rules

MMHB must be unable to do the following:

- **No infinite scroll / endless feed** — content is finite and self-paced.
- **No variable-reward loops** — no slot-machine reveals, no intermittent reinforcement.
- **No streaks-with-shame** — no "don't break your streak," no breakable counters used as pressure.
- **No loss-framing** — never "you'll fall behind / lose progress."
- **No manufactured urgency or scarcity** — no countdowns, no FOMO.
- **No engagement-bait notifications** — see non-coercive notification contract (`nervousSystemSafeUX.md`).
- **No roach-motel** — leaving, pausing, and turning things off are easy and honored.
- **Pacing & "enough for today"** — the app actively supports stopping; rest is celebrated.
- **Reduced-motion & calm defaults** — no attention-grabbing animation/autoplay.

---

## 3. Ethical retention systems

- **Earned, not coerced** — retention comes from real usefulness, trust, and respect.
- **Allowed:** genuine value, opt-in gentle invitations, respectful re-entry, dependable privacy.
- **Forbidden:** guilt, FOMO, streak-bait, manufactured urgency, hard-to-leave flows, engagement-maximizing AI tuning.
- **Graceful off-ramp** — MMHB supports a person needing it *less*; "leaving well" is a success metric, not churn-to-prevent-at-all-costs.

---

## 4. AI–human relationship boundaries

**The AI may:** mirror the person's own words, surface patterns they can choose to explore, offer optional non-clinical reflection prompts, point to in-app tools and crisis resources.

**The AI must never:**
- Diagnose, assess, or imply a clinical condition/outcome.
- Give treatment, medical, legal, or crisis-clinical advice.
- Persuade, upsell, or optimize for engagement/conversion.
- Foster unhealthy depend‑on‑the‑AI dynamics; it encourages real-world support and human connection.
- Take automated account actions based on reflections.
- Claim authority over the person's experience or replace human care.
- Continue normal flow on an explicit self-harm signal — it routes to crisis resources (BHCE) and defers to humans.

**Stance:** the AI is a *gentle mirror and librarian*, not a coach, therapist, friend-substitute, or judge. It actively points back toward the person's own wisdom and real-world relationships.

---

## 5. User trust architecture

- **Privacy-by-default** — reflections are the person's own; never sold, profiled for ads, or repurposed for persuasion.
- **Transparency** — plain-language "why you're seeing this" on derived content; honest about what MMHB is and isn't.
- **Consent-first** — opt-in for sensitive data; easy, total off-switches; reversible everywhere.
- **Honest claims** — educational, non-clinical, confidence-honest; no cure/outcome guarantees.
- **Safety-first** — crisis resources always in reach; safety overrides all engagement logic.
- **Predictability** — no surprise data use, no dark-pattern changes; trust is constant across the journey.

---

## 6. Healing vs. manipulation differentiation (engagement)

| Healing engagement (MMHB) | Manipulative engagement (rejected) |
|---|---|
| Serves wellbeing & agency | Extracts attention/money |
| Self-paced, finite | Infinite, compulsive |
| Calm, consent-based | Urgent, coercive |
| Easy exit, rest honored | Hard exit, guilt loops |
| Private, transparent | Profiled, opaque |
| Encourages real-world support | Fosters dependency |
| Crisis-first | Ignores/exploits crisis |

> This table is the operational test for any engagement mechanic: right column = does not ship.
