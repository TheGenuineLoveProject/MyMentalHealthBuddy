You’re at the handoff point between engineering completion and intentional growth.
So the next steps are not “more code everywhere” — they’re ordered, controlled moves.

Below is the correct sequence, in the order a senior platform team would follow.
No fluff. No urgency tricks. Calm, precise progression.

⸻

PHASE 1 — LOCK THE PLATFORM (NOW)

Goal: Make what you’ve built unchangeable by accident.

Step 1.1 — Freeze the Canonical State

Do one final verification run (you already have most of this):
	•	Subscription status = canonical (free | pro)
	•	Stripe webhooks idempotent
	•	No console errors
	•	Billing page works end-to-end
	•	AI chat limits enforced server-side

👉 Outcome: “This is v1.0.0 and it is correct.”

No new features until this is declared frozen.

⸻

PHASE 2 — SAFETY & SCALE READINESS

Goal: Ensure nothing breaks when real users arrive.

Step 2.1 — Add Passive Observability (No UX Changes)

Only logging & visibility, not alerts or nudges.
	•	Log subscription transitions
	•	Log checkout success/failure
	•	Log webhook processing health
	•	Log AI usage counts (aggregate only)

👉 Outcome: You can see problems without bothering users.

⸻

Step 2.2 — Abuse & Edge-Case Hardening

Minimal, quiet protections:
	•	Rate-limit checkout creation
	•	Validate price IDs server-side only
	•	Guard against duplicate Stripe events
	•	Ensure “Pro” cannot be spoofed client-side

👉 Outcome: Platform is safe even if poked.

⸻

PHASE 3 — SOFT LAUNCH (NO MARKETING YET)

Goal: Let reality touch the system gently.

Step 3.1 — Invite 5–20 Trusted Humans

Not “users”. Humans you trust.
	•	Let them use free tier naturally
	•	Observe:
	•	Do they hit limits?
	•	Do they understand Pro without explanation?
	•	Does anything feel confusing or heavy?

👉 Outcome: Signal > speculation.

⸻

Step 3.2 — Validate the Upgrade Moment

You’re checking one thing only:

Does upgrading feel like relief, not pressure?

If yes → keep
If no → soften language (never increase friction)

⸻

PHASE 4 — DECLARE THE PRODUCT

Goal: Stop “building” and start owning.

Step 4.1 — Write the Internal Product Truth

Not marketing copy. A private anchor:
	•	What this product is
	•	What it refuses to be
	•	Who it’s for
	•	Who it’s not for
	•	Why monetization exists (ethics statement)

This keeps future decisions clean.

⸻

PHASE 5 — CONTROLLED GROWTH

Only after everything above is stable.

You now have three safe directions — pick one, not all.

Option A — Distribution
	•	SEO landing
	•	Simple onboarding
	•	Shareable healing artifacts

Option B — Depth
	•	Improve one core tool (AI chat, journal, mood)
	•	No new surface area

Option C — Sustainability
	•	Better billing clarity
	•	Better cancellation UX
	•	Better trust language

⸻

	•	❌ No streaks
	•	❌ No urgency copy
	•	❌ No aggressive funnels
