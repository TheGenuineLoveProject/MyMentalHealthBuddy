2) SOCIAL WORK METHODS WITH EXAMPLES + SYSTEMS APPLICATION

A) Motivational Interviewing (MI) — Self-Reflection Version

Clarity Card (users see)
	•	What: A way of talking to yourself that helps you choose change, not force it.
	•	Who: Anyone who feels stuck, conflicted, or overwhelmed.
	•	When: Before a hard choice, after a slip, when motivation is low.
	•	Why: Pressure creates resistance. Choice creates movement.
	•	How: Ask → reflect → choose one tiny step.
	•	Where: “Change Builder” tools, onboarding, check-ins.

Beginner example (30 seconds)

Prompt: “On a 1–10 scale, how ready am I to try a tiny step?”
User answer: “4”
Follow-up: “What would make it a 5?”
Tiny step: “Put shoes by the door.”

Intermediate example (2–3 minutes)

Situation: “I want to journal but I avoid it.”
MI Script:
	•	“One reason I want to journal is ___.”
	•	“One reason I avoid it is ___.”
	•	“If I did the smallest version, it would be ___.”
Result: You pick the smallest doable action.

Advanced example (systems + relapse-safe)

Situation: “I keep repeating a pattern.”
	•	Change talk: “I want ___ because ___.”
	•	Sustain talk (allowed): “I don’t want ___ because ___.”
	•	Plan: “This week I’ll test one change: ___.”
	•	Review: “What did I learn? What stays? What changes?”

Where MI lives on the platform
	•	Onboarding: set goals with autonomy (“you choose”)
	•	Tools: prompts that evoke reasons
	•	Upgrades: consent-based CTAs (“if you want more structure…”)

B) Strengths-Based + Person-in-Environment (PIE)

Clarity Card
	•	What: A way to grow using what’s already working + your real-life context.
	•	Who: People who blame themselves too much.
	•	When: When you feel “broken” or “behind.”
	•	Why: Your environment affects behavior; growth becomes easier when the system supports it.
	•	How: Identify strengths → identify environment barriers → adjust the environment.
	•	Where: “Boundaries,” “Habits,” “Identity,” “Support Map.”

Beginner example

Question: “What’s one thing I’m already doing right?”
Answer: “I text my friend when I spiral.”
Strength: “I reach for connection.”
Next step: Put friend’s number in “Favorites.”

Intermediate example

Problem: “I can’t sleep.”
PIE check:
	•	Environment: caffeine, light, noise, screen time
	•	Support: one calming routine
	•	Strength: “I can commit when it’s simple.”
Action: “Set phone to night mode at 9pm.”

Advanced example (systems loop)
	•	Strength: “I do well with structure.”
	•	Barrier: “My schedule is chaotic.”
	•	System fix: “Create a 10-minute ‘anchor ritual’ that survives chaos.”
	•	Feedback: “Track what helps (not what’s perfect).”

Where PIE lives
	•	“Support Map” page
	•	“Environment Check” prompts inside every habit tool
	•	Dashboard “What supported you this week?” recap

3) NLP (ETHICAL) WITH CLEAR EXAMPLES (NO COERCION)

NLP Module: “Language → Attention → Action”

Clarity Card
	•	What: Using better wording so your brain can find better options.
	•	Who: Anyone stuck in harsh self-talk.
	•	When: When your mind says “I can’t / I’m broken / it’s hopeless.”
	•	Why: Words shape what you notice and what you attempt.
	•	How: Notice phrase → reframe → pick one observable step.
	•	Where: “Reframe Tool,” affirmations, identity builder.

Beginner example (reframe)

Old: “I’m failing.”

Next step: “Do the smallest version for 2 minutes.”

Intermediate example (identity statement)

Old: “I never follow through.”

Action: “Open journal and write one sentence.”

Advanced example (precision question)

Old: “I need to be better.”
Precision: “What does ‘better’ mean in one observable behavior today?”
Answer: “Drink water before coffee.”

5) SYSTEMS APPROACH — “THE GENUINE LOVE SYSTEM MAP”

This is the part that ties everything together so users understand how to apply it.

The 6-Part Systems Map (simple, powerful)

Every outcome is influenced by 6 interacting parts:
	1.	Body (sleep, food, movement, breath)
	2.	Mind (thoughts, attention, self-talk)
	3.	Emotions (signals, needs, regulation)
	4.	Behavior (habits, skills, routines)
	5.	Environment (people, space, time, money, stress)
	6.	Meaning (values, identity, purpose)

Beginner “system fix” example

	•	Body: drink water
	•	Mind: reframe one sentence
	•	Behavior: 60-second reset
	•	Environment: reduce noise
Result: small stabilizing shift

Intermediate example

Problem: “I keep procrastinating.”
	•	Mind: identify fear statement
	•	Behavior: 2-minute start rule
	•	Environment: remove one friction point
	•	Meaning: pick a value (“integrity”)
Result: behavior change without shame

Advanced example

Problem: “Recurring relationship pattern.”
	•	Emotions: name the need
	•	Environment: boundary change
	•	Meaning: values clarity
	•	Behavior: communication script
Result: pattern interruption
