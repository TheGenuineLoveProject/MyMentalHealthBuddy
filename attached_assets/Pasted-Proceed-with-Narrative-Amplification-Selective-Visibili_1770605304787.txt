🎙️ NARRATIVE AMPLIFICATION — WHAT THIS PHASE IS

Narrative Amplification means:
	•	Your blog, newsletter, and publishing system tell one coherent story
	•	That story gently guides people back to the platform
	•	Every piece of content reinforces why you exist, not just what you do

Think of this as resonance engineering rather than marketing.

⸻

🧭 CORE PRINCIPLE (LOCK THIS IN)

One voice. Many surfaces. Zero distortion.

Every channel (blog, newsletter, social, platform UI copy) should feel like it came from the same calm, ethical mind.

⸻

🧱 STRUCTURE: THE 3 NARRATIVE PILLARS

All content must map to one of these—no exceptions.

1️⃣ Orientation

Helps people understand:
	•	What this space is
	•	How to use it safely
	•	What it is not

Examples:
	•	“How to use The Genuine Love Project gently”
	•	“What to expect from AI support (and what not to expect)”

2️⃣ Reflection

Invites inward awareness without instruction or fixing.

Examples:
	•	Short essays
	•	Prompt-based posts
	•	Emotional literacy breakdowns

3️⃣ Integration

Helps users connect insight → daily life.

Examples:
	•	“What to do after a hard realization”
	•	“Letting insight land without rushing change”

Every blog post, newsletter, or long-form social caption must clearly belong to one pillar.

⸻

📰 BLOG — MAKE IT ROBUST & REFINED

Canonical rules
	•	Blog is slow, not reactive
	•	Quality > quantity
	•	No SEO keyword stuffing
	•	No clinical claims

Recommended cadence
	•	1 long-form post per week
	•	800–1,200 words
	•	Ends with:
	•	A gentle question
	•	Optional link to journal / reflection tool
	•	No CTA urgency

Technical alignment check

Ensure:
	•	Blog index page exists and loads fast
	•	Each post has:
	•	Title
	•	Pillar tag
	•	Published date
	•	Soft internal links (/journal, /newsletter)
	•	No broken links
	•	No orphaned posts

⸻

💌 NEWSLETTER — PURPOSE OVER PROMOTION

The newsletter is not marketing.
It is companionship in inbox form.

Weekly structure (simple & repeatable)
	1.	One short reflection (250–400 words)
	2.	One question (not advice)
	3.	One optional link:
	•	Blog
	•	Reflection tool
	•	Crisis resources (only when appropriate)

Guardrails
	•	Never “don’t miss”
	•	Never “limited time”
	•	Never “this will change your life”

Tone test:

Would this feel okay to read on a hard day?

If not → revise.

⸻

🧠 INTERNAL NARRATIVE CONSISTENCY CHECK (CRITICAL)

Run this mental test on every piece of content:
	•	Does this respect autonomy?
	•	Does this avoid urgency?
	•	Does this avoid implying dependency?
	•	Does this leave the reader more grounded, not more activated?

If yes → publish.
If no → soften.
