# Newsletter Style Guide

## Purpose
The newsletter is companionship in inbox form — not marketing.

## Frequency
- No fixed schedule required
- Send when there is something meaningful to share
- Recommended: no more than once per week
- If nothing meaningful this week, don't send

## Tone
- Calm, warm, grounded
- "You might" not "you should"
- "If you'd like" not "you need to"
- "Whenever feels right" not "don't miss this"

## Structure (Every Email)
1. One short reflection (250-400 words)
2. One question (not advice)
3. One optional link (blog, journal, reflection, or crisis)
4. Crisis-safe footer (always)

## Language Rules

### Always Use
- Explore, reflect, notice, practice, welcome
- Gentle, private, at your pace, whenever you're ready
- "This is offered, not required"

### Never Use
- Heal (as a promise), cure, fix, transform (as guarantee)
- Urgent, exclusive, limited time, don't miss out
- You should, you must, you need to, act now
- Last chance, before it's too late, today only

## The Hard-Day Test
Before sending any email, ask:

> "Would this feel okay to read on a hard day?"

If the answer is no — revise until it is yes.

## Ethical Guardrails
- No engagement metrics shown to readers
- No "you haven't opened our emails" re-engagement
- No guilt about not reading previous emails
- No countdown timers or artificial scarcity
- No tracking pixels beyond basic open rates
- Unsubscribe is always one click, no guilt

## Templates
- Welcome: `welcome-template.md`
- Weekly: `weekly-template.md`
- Footer: `crisis-safe-footer.md`
- Drafts: `drafts.json`

## Sending Process
1. Write draft following weekly-template.md
2. Run through publish checklist (NARRATIVE_SPINE.md Section J)
3. Test-send to admin email first
4. Review on mobile and desktop
5. Send manually — never auto-send
