# 4-Week Newsletter Starter

Four weekly issues to ship in the first month of public launch. Each issue is short on purpose: one theme, one tool, one ask. Average read time per issue: under two minutes.

**Subject-line rule**: never use urgency, never use the word *finally*, never promise outcomes.
**Footer rule**: every issue ends with the crisis line, then a single soft CTA to `/start`.

## Week 1 — "One Slow Breath"

**Module**: `anxiety` · **Tool**: `box_breathing`
**Subject**: One slow breath this week
**Preheader**: A 60-second tool you can use anywhere.

Hi,

Welcome. This is the first issue of the MMHB letter. Each week, one tool, one short read, one thing you can try in under two minutes.

This week, the tool is the simplest one we have: a box breath. Four seconds in, four hold, four out, four hold. Sixteen seconds is one round. Most people feel a small change after the second round, not the first.

Here's why it works: anxiety is your nervous system running ahead of the room. A structured breath is a small repeating signal that says: *someone is here, the next sixteen seconds are accounted for*. You don't have to feel calmer. You only have to give your body the shape.

Try it once today, before you read another email. Notice what changes. Notice what doesn't. Both are useful.

If you want to try it with a guide instead of a script, our /start page will walk you through one round.

Take care of yourself this week,
The MMHB team

## Week 2 — "When the Thought Lands Hard"

**Module**: `thought_pattern` · **Tool**: `thought_reframe`
**Subject**: A thought is a draft, not a verdict
**Preheader**: A four-step move for the sentence that won't leave your head.

Hi,

This week, the tool is for the thought that lands and stays. *I'm going to lose this. I always mess this up. They don't actually like me.* Sentences that arrive with the weight of facts and start organizing your day before you've had coffee.

The move is a four-step reframe:

1. Catch the thought, in the exact words it arrived in.
2. Underline the absolute words: *always, never, can't, no one, everyone*. They're usually where the draft is overwriting reality.
3. Write a smaller, truer version. Not the cheerful one. The accurate one.
4. Notice what changes in your body. Sometimes nothing. Sometimes a small unclench.

What this won't do: it won't argue you out of grief, or fear that's appropriate, or the actual hard thing in front of you. Reframing is for the layer of suffering you added on top, not for the suffering itself.

Try it once this week with one sentence that's been following you around. The /start page has a guided card if you'd like the steps held for you.

Take care,
The MMHB team

## Week 3 — "Name It, Then Locate It"

**Module**: `emotional_processing` · **Tool**: `emotional_checkin`
**Subject**: A check-in that takes one minute
**Preheader**: The two-step move that does most of the work.

Hi,

This week, a small daily-practice tool. The full move is *name it, then locate it*.

Three lines:

- *I'm feeling __________.*
- *It lives in my __________.*
- *It feels like __________.*

That's the whole exercise. You don't have to do anything with the answer. The naming and the locating, together, are the practice.

Why locating matters: feelings are not abstract weather. They live somewhere in your body. When you name a feeling but skip the body, you stay in your head, narrating the feeling instead of meeting it.

If "I can't name it" is what comes up, try the smaller question first: *pleasant or unpleasant? big or small?* That's enough to start.

If a feeling becomes overwhelming, please reach out — the line is below.

Try the check-in once this week. Same time of day if you can. The /start page has a one-screen version if you'd like structure.

Take care,
The MMHB team

## Week 4 — "The Repair Is the Relationship"

**Module**: `relationship` · **Tool**: `relationship_repair`
**Subject**: After the rupture
**Preheader**: A short script for a hard conversation.

Hi,

Most people think relationships are made or broken by the absence of conflict. They aren't. They're shaped by what happens after the conflict.

The opening move of repair is short. It doesn't require agreement. It doesn't require a perfect apology. It requires showing up.

Try this script next time you need it:

- *I've been thinking about what happened between us.*
- *Here's what I want to be true between us: __________.*
- *Here's the part I want to take responsibility for: __________.*
- *I'd like to hear how it landed for you, when you're ready.*

A few things to notice:
- There's no defending in the script. Defenses can come later.
- Repair doesn't always land the first time. The second attempt usually does.
- Some ruptures shouldn't be repaired by you alone. Patterns of harm need different help.

If you want to walk through a repair with structure, our /start page has a guided card.

Thank you for reading the first month of this letter. Next month we'll go deeper into pattern interrupts and self-regulation.

Take care,
The MMHB team
