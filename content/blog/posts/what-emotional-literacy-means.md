Emotional literacy is not about controlling your feelings. It is about recognizing them.

Most of us were never taught how to name what we feel. We learned "fine" and "not fine." We learned to push through, distract ourselves, or explain away discomfort. But emotional literacy is the practice of noticing — gently — what is actually happening inside you.

## What it looks like in practice

- Pausing when you feel tension and asking: "What is this?"
- Noticing that frustration might actually be grief wearing a different face
- Letting sadness exist without rushing to solve it
- Recognizing when your body is telling you something your mind has not caught up to yet

## What it does not look like

- Labeling yourself ("I am an anxious person")
- Forcing positive thinking over real feelings
- Labeling yourself through internet checklists
- Believing you should always know exactly what you feel

Emotional literacy is a practice, not a test. Some days you will notice clearly. Other days everything will feel tangled. Both are valid.

## A small experiment

If you would like to try something gentle: the next time you feel a shift in your mood, pause for five seconds and try to name what you feel. Not to fix it. Just to notice it.

You might be surprised by what you find.
