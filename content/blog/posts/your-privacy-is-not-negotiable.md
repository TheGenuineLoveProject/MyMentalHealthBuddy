Most apps ask you to trade privacy for convenience. We chose not to.

When we built The Genuine Love Project, we made a decision early on: your reflections, your journal entries, your emotional check-ins — none of it would be used to sell ads, train third-party models, or build a profile of you for marketing purposes.

## What privacy means here

- Your journal entries belong to you. We do not read them, analyze them for marketing, or share them with anyone.
- Your mood data is not aggregated or sold. It exists to help you notice your own patterns — nothing else.
- There are no social features that expose your inner world. No public profiles, no follower counts, no "share your progress" nudges.
- You can leave at any time. There is no lock-in, no guilt about leaving, and no "we miss you" emails designed to pull you back.

## Why this matters for wellness

Emotional exploration requires safety. If you are worried about who might see your reflections, you will not reflect honestly. Privacy is not a feature here — it is the foundation.

## What we will never do

- Sell your data to advertisers or data brokers
- Use your reflections to target you with content
- Create engagement traps designed to keep you scrolling
- Send manipulative re-engagement emails

## A quiet promise

We would rather have fewer users who trust us deeply than millions who feel watched.
