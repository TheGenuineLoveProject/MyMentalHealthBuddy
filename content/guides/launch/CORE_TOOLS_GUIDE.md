# Core Tools Guide

Onboarding copy for the six core tools shipped in the product. Use this guide as the source of truth for in-app tool descriptions, marketing pages, and help-center entries. Each entry is short on purpose: 100-150 words, one image of the user holding it, one entry-point link.

---

## 1. Box Breathing

**Module**: `anxiety` · **Tool id**: `box_breathing` · **Duration**: ~60-90 seconds

What it is: a breath with a shape. Four seconds in, four hold, four out, four hold. One round is sixteen seconds. You can do one round and stop.

When to use it: any time your body feels ahead of the room. Before a hard conversation. After bad news. In the parking lot before walking inside. It works whether or not you believe it will.

What it isn't: a fix. It doesn't change the situation that triggered the spike. It changes the body that's about to respond to it.

## 2. 5-4-3-2-1 Grounding

**Module**: `anxiety` (safety pairing) · **Tool id**: `grounding_54321` · **Duration**: ~60 seconds

What it is: a sensory anchor. Name 5 things you can see, 4 you can hear, 3 you can touch, 2 you can smell, 1 you can taste. The order matters less than the noticing.

When to use it: when your mind is somewhere else and you need to be back in the room. When a memory is louder than the present. Before sleep, when thoughts won't slow.

What it isn't: a replacement for trauma processing. This is a daily-use anchor, not a treatment.

## 3. Thought Reframe

**Module**: `thought_pattern` · **Tool id**: `thought_reframe` · **Duration**: ~2 minutes

What it is: a four-step pass over a thought that's organizing your day uninvited. Catch the sentence. Underline the absolutes. Write a smaller, truer version. Notice what changes.

When to use it: when a single sentence is following you around. *I always mess this up. They don't actually like me. I'm going to lose this.* Sentences that arrive with the weight of facts.

What it isn't: positive thinking. The truer version is rarely the cheerful version. It's the accurate one.

Try it now → /start

## 4. Emotional Check-In

**Module**: `emotional_processing` · **Tool id**: `emotional_checkin` · **Duration**: ~1 minute

What it is: name the feeling, locate it in your body, describe how it feels. Three lines. That's the whole exercise.

When to use it: as a daily practice, not a crisis tool. Same time of day if you can. Brushing-your-teeth cadence.

What it isn't: a substitute for therapy, and not a tool for processing trauma. Trauma processing is slower, structured, and usually held by a professional.

Try it now → /start

## 5. Relationship Repair

**Module**: `relationship` · **Tool id**: `relationship_repair` · **Duration**: ~3 minutes

What it is: a short script for the moment after a rupture with someone you want to keep. Four lines, in order: I've been thinking about us. Here's what I want to be true between us. Here's the part I want to take responsibility for. I'd like to hear how it landed for you.

When to use it: small, normal ruptures with people you care about.

What it isn't: a tool for ongoing harm or controlling dynamics. Those need different help.

Try it now → /start

## 6. Pattern Interrupt

**Module**: `loop_detection` · **Tool id**: `pattern_interrupt` · **Duration**: ~30-60 seconds

What it is: a ten-second move to break a loop. Stand up. Ask quietly: *what am I actually looking for?* Pick a smaller version of the answer.

When to use it: doomscrolling. The third bag of chips. The sixteenth refresh of an inbox. Anything your hands are doing that your mind didn't decide.

What it isn't: a willpower test. The loop isn't proof you're broken. It's a pattern with an entry point.

Try it now → /start

## 7. Overload Reset

**Module**: `self_regulation` · **Tool id**: `overload_reset` · **Duration**: ~5 minutes

What it is: a structured five-minute sequence for moments when there are too many open tabs in your nervous system. Body scan, breath, one decision, one closure.

When to use it: end of a heavy day. Mid-task when the room blurs. Before bed when sleep won't come.

What it isn't: a productivity hack. The point isn't to get more done. The point is to be in your body when you do it.

## How to Surface These Tools in Marketing

- **Always link to `/start`**, never directly to a paywall or signup gate.
- **Always describe the tool in user-language**, not in clinical-mechanism language. *"A breath with a shape"* not *"diaphragmatic regulation of vagal tone."*
- **Always include the duration up front.** Time is the currency. Promising 60 seconds and delivering 90 is fine. Promising 60 and delivering 4 minutes is not.
- **Always say what the tool isn't.** This is the trust move. It's also the legal one.
